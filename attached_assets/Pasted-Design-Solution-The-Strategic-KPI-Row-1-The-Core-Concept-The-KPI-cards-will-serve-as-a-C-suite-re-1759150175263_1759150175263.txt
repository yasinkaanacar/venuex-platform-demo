Design Solution: The Strategic KPI Row
1. The Core Concept

The KPI cards will serve as a C-suite-ready snapshot of omnichannel performance. They must be instantly scannable and answer the highest-level question: "What was the result of our investment?" We will design a row of four interconnected cards, each representing a pillar of performance: Efficiency (ROAS), Value (Revenue), Volume (Conversions), and Cost (Cost per Conversion).

The key innovation is that each card will not just be a static number. It will be a self-contained story, showing the primary metric, its trend versus a previous period, and a visual sparkline chart for at-a-glance momentum.

2. Visual Mockup & Design Specification

The component is a single horizontal row of four Card elements, designed to be the first thing a user analyzes after the global date filter.

Component Breakdown: The Anatomy of a Single KPI Card

Each of the four cards will share the exact same structure for consistency and scannability.

Title: A clear, concise label at the top (e.g., "Offline ROAS").

Primary Metric: A large, bold, attention-grabbing number. This is the hero of the card.

Comparison Metric (The "Delta"): Directly below the primary metric, this shows the percentage change compared to the previous period. It uses a colored icon (green ▲ for positive change, red ▼ for negative) for immediate comprehension.

Sparkline Chart: A small, simple line chart at the bottom of the card. It visualizes the trend of the primary metric over the selected date range, providing crucial context about momentum (e.g., "Is our ROAS stable, growing, or declining?").

The Four Cards:

Card 1: Offline ROAS (The Efficiency Pillar)

Title: Offline ROAS

Primary Metric: 4.6x

Comparison Metric: ▲ 15% vs. previous 30 days

Sparkline: A line showing the daily or weekly ROAS trend.

Card 2: Attributed Offline Revenue (The Value Pillar)

Title: Attributed Offline Revenue

Primary Metric: ₺1.2M

Comparison Metric: ▲ 8% vs. previous 30 days

Sparkline: A line showing the revenue trend.

Card 3: Attributed Conversions (The Volume Pillar)

Title: Attributed Conversions

Primary Metric: 2,150

Comparison Metric: ▲ 12% vs. previous 30 days

Sparkline: A line showing the trend of total sales transactions.

Card 4: Cost Per Store Conversion (The Cost Pillar)

Title: Cost Per Store Conversion

Primary Metric: ₺45.50

Comparison Metric: ▼ 5% vs. previous 30 days (Note: A decrease is positive here, so it would be styled in green).

Sparkline: A line showing the cost efficiency trend.

3. Interaction Design: The Explanatory Tooltip

To maintain a clean interface while providing deeper insight, we will add a detailed tooltip on hover.

Functionality: When a user hovers their mouse over any of the four KPI cards, a Tooltip component will appear.

Tooltip Content: The tooltip will provide a breakdown of the primary metric by the most logical dimension: Platform. This gives the user an immediate, secondary level of analysis without needing to consult the main table.

Example Tooltip (when hovering over the "Attributed Offline Revenue" card):

Revenue by Platform
• Google Ads: ₺700,000
• Meta: ₺450,000
• TikTok: ₺50,000

This interaction provides a powerful "progressive disclosure" of information, keeping the dashboard clean by default but offering valuable detail on demand.

4. Why This Solution is Ideal

High Information Density: It packs four critical pieces of information (metric, delta, period, trend) into a very small, elegant space, respecting the user's time.

C-Suite Ready: The focus on strategic, financial outcomes (ROAS, Revenue) speaks directly to the needs of the CMO and other executive stakeholders.

Instantly Scannable: The consistent layout, combined with color-coding and icons, allows a user to assess the overall health of their omnichannel efforts in under five seconds.

Contextualizes Data: A static number is meaningless without context. The delta comparison and the sparkline trend immediately tell the user whether a number is "good" or "bad" and in which direction it's moving, prompting further investigation in the charts and tables below.

Action-Oriented: A card showing a declining ROAS with a downward-trending sparkline is a powerful visual alert that immediately signals a problem that needs to be diagnosed using the other components on the page.
