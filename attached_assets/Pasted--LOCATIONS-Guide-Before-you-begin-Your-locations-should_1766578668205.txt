# LOCATIONS — Guide

## Before you begin
- Your locations should be managed under a single Google Business Profile (GBP) group/account to avoid split ownership and missing locations.
- You will need someone who can grant **Owner-level access** in GBP (Manager access is not enough in many cases).
- If you plan to sync to Apple, you must have an **Apple Business Connect (ABC)** account (or be ready to create one).

## Step 1 — Your Address Data
To import and match locations reliably:
- Each store should have a unique **Store Code** (your ERP/store identifier).
- The same Store Code should be used consistently across all files you share (locations, sales, inventory).

## Step 2 — Where to Sync
Choose where VenueX will publish/update your location data:
- Google (GBP)
- Apple (ABC) — requires an ABC account
- Yandex (optional)

## Step 3 — Review & Connect
What happens after you connect GBP:
- VenueX will automatically import your location information.
- After import, Review Management can be enabled with a single action.

Tip: You can pause and continue later — your Integration ID keeps your progress.

# OFFLINE SALES — Guide

## Before you begin
- VenueX ingests data via **SFTP/FTP**. During setup, we’ll ask for a **file/folder URL**.
- File format must be **CSV (UTF-8)**.
- Recommended frequency is **daily** (minimum weekly). Fresher data improves attribution quality.
- We never ask for raw PII. If you include identifiers (email/phone), they must be **SHA-256 hashed on your side**.

## Step 1 — Your Offline Data (what your export must include)
Required fields:
- Country
- Conversion Name
- Conversion Time (ISO-8601)
- Conversion Value
- Currency

Strongly recommended (for matching):
- Email (lowercase, trimmed) — SHA-256 hashed
- Phone (E.164 format, e.g. +90...) — SHA-256 hashed

Optional but useful:
- Store Code (must be consistent across all files)
- Order ID (unique transaction ID)

## Step 2 — Build Mapping
To avoid “data received but not matched” issues:
- Email must be normalized (lowercase/trim) before hashing.
- Phone must be normalized to E.164 before hashing.
- Dates must be ISO-8601 (including timezone).

## Step 3 — Review & Save
- You can backfill offline sales data up to **90 days** (useful for pilots and validation).

# LOCAL INVENTORY — Guide

## Before you begin
Local Inventory requires store-level availability. Make sure you can export:
- Product ID (must match your Merchant Center product identifier)
- Store Code (same standard as Locations & Offline Sales)
- Availability (in_stock / out_of_stock)

Optional fields (recommended if available):
- Price (if store price differs from online)
- Quantity

## Step 1 — Your Inventory Data
- Store Code must match your Locations store code format exactly.
- Product ID must match the ID used in your product catalog (Merchant Center alignment).

## Step 2 — Build Mapping
- Inventory data is also delivered via **SFTP/FTP** as **CSV (UTF-8)**.

## Step 3 — Review & Save
- Daily updates are strongly recommended to keep availability accurate.
