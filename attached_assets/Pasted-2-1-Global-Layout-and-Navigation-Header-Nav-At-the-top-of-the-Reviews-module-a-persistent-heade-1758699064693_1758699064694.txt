2.4 Products Section

Purpose: The Products section mirrors Locations but for product-specific reviews. This is particularly useful if VenueX’s clients also track reviews of products or SKUs (for retailers, think product ratings on their site or aggregated from places like Google Merchant or Amazon if applicable). It allows a Product Ops or Brand Manager to see how each product is rated and what feedback it’s getting, across all locations and channels.

Layout & Components:

Products Table: A listing of products (or product categories) with columns similar to Locations: Product Name (and maybe SKU or category), Average Rating (if numerical ratings exist for products), Review Count, Sentiment summary (if textual feedback), and possibly Common Pros/Cons for that product. For example, “Product XYZ – 4.5★ – 120 reviews – Top Compliment: ‘quality’, Top Complaint: ‘price’.” If clients integrate e-commerce reviews, this is where those appear. If we are instead deriving product mentions from location reviews (e.g. “the shoes I bought were great”), then this section might be more about topics. However, since the spec mentions product→SKU hierarchy, likely clients have direct product reviews we ingest (maybe via Bazaarvoice or Trustpilot). So each row is basically the aggregate of that product’s reviews.

Category Filter: Likely a filter to view by category or brand line. E.g. filter to “Laptops” to see all laptop products’ ratings.

Product Detail: Clicking a product row would show that product’s detail page: overall rating, a breakdown of ratings by location (if we know which store sold it or review by store), and common themes in its reviews. For instance, “Camera X: 4.2★ – strong praise for image quality, complaints about battery life” discovered via text mining. This detail would help Product Ops or merchandisers identify if a product has a design issue or if its sentiment is improving after a new version.

Integration with Inventory: Because VenueX ties to offline data, we could also show how many sold or returned – but that might be beyond just reviews. At minimum, we show how reviews for that product trending over time (especially around launch dates). If integrated with knowledge graph, maybe show product attributes (release date, etc.) for context.

Review List by Product: Could also list a few recent reviews for that product (like a mini-inbox filtered to that product). If someone from product team wants to respond (in some contexts, product managers respond to e-commerce reviews or community feedback), they can do so from here or jump to Inbox with a product filter.

UX Flow: A Product Manager logs in and goes to Products. They see a list of their portfolio with ratings. One product stands out with lower score than others. They click it to examine reviews and find a recurring complaint about sizing. They then coordinate with design to fix that. Or they notice an improvement in ratings after a known issue was resolved – validating their actions. For a CMO, they could filter to a category to see how a new line is being received by customers across all stores. The design thereby provides an actionable view for product improvements, aligning with the "valuable" part – e.g. showing receding themes might highlight that a previous problem (like “battery overheating”) no longer appears in recent reviews, which is a win. If product reviews are not a major focus or available data for a client, this section can be hidden or repurposed (the UI should be modular). But given spec calls it out, we design it fully.
