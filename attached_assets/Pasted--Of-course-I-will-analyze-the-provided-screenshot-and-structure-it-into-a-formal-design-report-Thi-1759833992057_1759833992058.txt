Design Report: Geographic Performance Dashboard
1. The Core Concept

This dashboard is designed to provide a comprehensive, location-based view of omnichannel marketing performance. Its primary goal is to answer the strategic question: "How does our advertising performance and its resulting revenue differ across various geographic regions?"

The design achieves this by combining high-level data visualization with a granular data table. The top section uses choropleth maps for an instant, visual understanding of performance distribution, while the bottom section provides a detailed data table for deep, quantifiable analysis. This dual approach serves both the executive who needs a quick summary (the maps) and the analyst who needs to investigate the underlying numbers (the table).

2. Visual Mockup & Component Breakdown

The dashboard is composed of three primary components: a filter bar, two map visualizations, and a detailed performance table.

Component 1: The Geographic Filter Bar

Description: Two dropdown menus, Country and State, located at the top of the dashboard.

Purpose: To allow users to drill down from a national overview to a more specific regional or state-level analysis.

Functionality: Applying a filter in this bar will update both the map visualizations and the data table below to reflect the selected geographic area. The State filter is likely dependent on the Country selection.

Component 2: The Map Visualizations

Description: Two side-by-side choropleth maps of Turkey. A choropleth map uses different shades of a color to represent the intensity of a metric in a specific geographic area.

Purpose: To provide an immediate, scannable, and intuitive visual representation of where performance is strongest and weakest.

Map 1: Omni Revenue Distribution

Metric: Total Omnichannel Revenue (Online + Offline).

Visualization: Uses a blue color scale. Darker shades of blue indicate states with higher total revenue, while lighter shades indicate lower revenue. A legend at the bottom (37,956 to 169,173,725) quantifies the color scale.

Map 2: Omni ROAS Distribution

Metric: Total Omnichannel ROAS (Return on Ad Spend).

Visualization: Uses a green color scale. Darker shades of green indicate states with higher ROAS (better efficiency), while lighter shades indicate lower ROAS. A legend at the bottom (15.4 to 158.4) quantifies the color scale.

Component 3: The Geographic Performance Table

Description: A detailed data table located below the maps, presenting the raw numbers that power the visualizations.

Purpose: To enable deep-dive analysis, allowing users to compare specific states side-by-side across a wide range of performance metrics.

Data Fields / Columns:

Country, State: Identifiers for the geographic location.

Spends: The total advertising investment in that region.

Interactions: A top-of-funnel engagement metric.

Store Visits: An estimated metric for physical foot traffic.

Website Visits: The volume of online traffic.

Online Transactions, Offline Transactions: The volume of sales broken down by channel.

Online Revenue, Offline Revenue: The monetary value of sales broken down by channel.

Online ROAS, Offline ROAS: The efficiency of ad spend for each channel.

AOV (Average Order Value): The average transaction value.

Multiplier: A custom metric, likely representing the lift or relationship between online and offline performance.

3. Interaction Design

Map Tooltips: Hovering over a state on either map should trigger a tooltip that displays the state's name and the exact value of the metric being visualized (e.g., "Istanbul: ₺169,173,725 Revenue").

Cross-Component Filtering (Click Interaction): Clicking on a state in either map should filter the Performance Table below to show data for only that selected state.

Table Sorting and Pagination: All columns in the table should be sortable. The pagination controls (1 - 81 / 81, < >) at the bottom right indicate that the table can handle large datasets.

Table Toolbar: The icons (⋮, ☶) suggest a toolbar for table actions, which could include exporting the data (e.g., to CSV) or customizing the visible columns.

4. Why This Design is Effective

Caters to Multiple Personas: The visual maps are perfect for executives who need a quick, high-level understanding of regional performance. The detailed table serves the data analyst who needs to dig into the numbers and understand the "why" behind the trends.

High Information Density: The dashboard successfully presents a large number of complex metrics in a structured and digestible format. The combination of visuals and raw data provides a complete picture.

Connects Investment to Outcome: The table clearly links Spends (the investment) to both online and offline Revenue and ROAS (the outcomes) on a geographic basis, which is a core requirement for any performance marketing dashboard.

Highlights Omnichannel Value: By explicitly breaking out metrics like "Online Revenue" and "Offline Revenue," the design effectively showcases the distinct value of each channel and validates the need for an omnichannel measurement strategy.
